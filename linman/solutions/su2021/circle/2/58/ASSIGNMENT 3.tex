Draw a  pair of tangents to a circle of radius 5 units  which are inclined to each other at an angle of $60\degree$

\\
Let $PA$ and $PB$  be tangents  to circle with radius 5 cm  which are inclined to each other at an angle $60\degree$.
\\
We know a tangent is always perpendicular to the radius .
\begin{align}
\therefore OA \perp AP ,  OB \perp BP.
\\
\angle OAP=90\degree, \angle OBP=90\degree.
\end{align}
We know that, line joining the centre and the external point bisect the angle between pair of tangents from that external point.
\\
$\angle APB=\theta =60\degree$
\\
OP bisect the angle \theta=60\degree
\\
 In  $\triangle OAP$, 
\begin{align}
\sin\frac{\theta}{2}=\frac{OA}{OP}
\\
\frac{1}{2}=\frac{5}{OP}
\\
\implies OP=10
\\
\therefore \vec{O}=\myvec{0\\0} , \vec{P}=\myvec{10\\0}
\end{align}
Now, 
\begin{align}
\angle AOB + \angle OBP + \angle APB + \angle PAO=360\degree
\\
\implies \angle AOB +90\degree + 60\degree +90\degree =360\degree
\\
\angle AOB= 360\degree -140\degree
\\
\angle AOB =120\degree.
\\
From, this
\\
\angle AOP=60\degree
\end{align}
\begin{lemma}
\label{lemma}
The coordinates of $\vec{A}$ and  $\vec{B}$ can be written as follows:
\begin{align}
 \vec{A} =O+r \myvec{\cos \theta\\\sin \theta}
 \\
 \vec{B} =O+r \myvec{\cos (360-\theta)\\\sin (360-\theta )}
 \\
 \vec{B} =O+r \myvec{\cos\theta\\-\sin\theta}
\end{align}
Proof:
Now substitute the values , 
we get
\item For finding coordinate of A:-
\begin{align}
&\implies \vec{A}=\myvec{0\\0}+5\myvec{\cos 60 \\\sin 60 }
&\implies \vec{A}=\myvec{2.5\\4.33}
\end{align}
\item For finding the coordinate of B:
\begin{align}
\vec{B}=\myvec{0\\0}+5\myvec{\cos 60\\-\sin60}
\implies \vec{B}=\myvec{2.5\\-4.33}
\\
\therefore \vec{O}=\myvec{0\\0}, \vec{P}=\myvec{10\\0}, \vec{A}=\myvec{2.5\\4.33}, \vec{B}=\myvec{2.5\\-4.33}.
\end{align}
